The product

Six modules.
Every compensation question, answered.

We didn't build CompIQ to display data. We built it to answer the questions HR leaders actually get asked — by their CEO, their CFO, and their best people. Each module exists for a specific question. Nothing is decorative.

01
Pay positioning

Are we paying competitively?

The question every CEO asks before approving a merit cycle. The histogram view shows your full population compared against the market median, banded by compa-ratio. Below 0.85 means you're losing people. Above 1.15 means you're overpaying for what you have. Both are problems CompIQ surfaces immediately.

  • → Compa-ratio distribution across the full org
  • → Pay positioning by division, level, function, geography
  • → Quarter-over-quarter movement, with explanations
Sample insight

"Engineering is paying at the 23rd percentile of market. Three senior roles are now at significant flight risk."

— Q1 quarterly review, sample client
02
Role explorer

What should we pay this person?

The workhorse of CompIQ. Search any employee, role, or level. See the market range, your internal peers, the recommended action — and click through to the full employee profile with tenure, last raise, performance flag, and retention modeling.

  • → Live filter by division, level, country, compa band
  • → Employee detail drawer with full comp history
  • → Recommended action ranked by business impact
Use it for
  • Counter-offer prep. Before the resignation lands.
  • New hire offers. Anchored in market, not anchored in the last person you hired.
  • Promotion decisions. What does this role pay at the next level?
  • CEO ad-hoc questions. "What does Sarah make and is it right?"
03
Geographic intelligence

Are we paying the Toronto team correctly?

A world map with country bubbles sized by headcount and colored by compensation health. Multi-state pay transparency for the US. Localized cost-of-labor data from Salary.com — not crude cost-of-living adjustments that don't reflect what the market actually pays.

  • → Country-level and state-level localization
  • → Pay equity surfaced by region
  • → Remote vs in-office market deltas
The hard question

When pay transparency laws hit your state, will you be defensible?

Twelve US states now require salary ranges in job postings. CompIQ builds those ranges for you — and shows whether your internal pay supports them.

04
Pay equity

Where are our gaps — before they become lawsuits?

Pay equity ranked ascending by division and region, so the gaps surface first. A tenure heatmap reveals where new hires are out-earning loyal employees. Issues are flagged in plain language with specific recommended fixes.

  • → Division-level and region-level equity scoring
  • → Tenure-vs-pay heatmap
  • → Suggested adjustments with cost estimates
Don't get caught flat-footed

Most equity issues don't show up in the quarterly P&L. They show up the day a high performer asks why the person hired six months ago — into the same role — is making twenty thousand dollars more.

CompIQ surfaces those before the conversation does.

05
Flight risk

Who's the next resignation — and what does it cost to keep them?

A composite retention score combines pay position, tenure curve, peer comp velocity, and performance signal. The top 30 retention targets surface with cost-to-retain modeling per person — including what it would cost to do nothing.

  • → Composite retention risk score, 0–100
  • → Cost-to-retain modeled per employee
  • → Replacement cost benchmark by role
The math

Most replacements cost 12× the retention adjustment that would have prevented them.

Retention is rarely a budget conversation. It's an accounting conversation — and CompIQ does that math for you.

06
Quarterly insights

What does all of this mean?

Software doesn't render judgment. We do. Every quarter, your CompIQ consultant writes a short editorial-style read on your data — what changed, what to do about it, what to bring to your CEO. Signed by name. Not generated.

  • → Quarterly written review by your named consultant
  • → Three to five prioritized actions per quarter
  • → Ad-hoc questions answered in-platform
This is the moat

Pave can't write you a quarterly read. Mercer can — for a quarter of a million dollars. CompIQ does it because Stuart and the senior advisory team sit on the other side of the dashboard.

That's the entire reason CompIQ exists.
